Day 1–10: Easy → Confidence Builders

Focus: clarity, structure, calm delivery
	1.	If you had to give up one daily habit, what would it be and why?
	2.	What does success mean to you today?
	3.	What is one lesson school never taught you but life did?
	4.	If you could relive one day, which day would it be?
	5.	What is your favorite way to relax after a stressful day?
	6.	Who has influenced you the most in your life?
	7.	What is one thing you are grateful for right now?
	8.	If money was not a concern, how would you spend your time?
	9.	What is a small habit that changed your life?
	10.	What does “home” mean to you?


Day 11–20: Medium → Thinking & Opinion

Focus: viewpoints, examples, reasoning
	11.	Is failure more important than success? Why?
	12.	Should leaders be feared or loved?
	13.	What is more powerful: discipline or motivation?
	14.	Does technology bring people closer or push them apart?
	15.	Is honesty always the best policy?
	16.	What matters more: talent or hard work?
	17.	Should children be taught money management in school?
	18.	Is change exciting or scary for you?
	19.	Can silence be more powerful than words?
	20.	What defines a good leader?


Day 21–25: Advanced → Abstract & Creative

Focus: originality, metaphors, depth
	21.	If life had a “pause” button, when would you press it?
	22.	What would you tell your 18-year-old self?
	23.	If your life were a book, what would this chapter be called?
	24.	If emotions had colors, what color is today?
	25.	If success made a sound, what would it be?


Day 26–30: Competition-Level → Twist & Pressure

Focus: surprise handling, confidence, humor
	26.	Speak for something you secretly disagree with.
	27.	Defend a failure as if it were a success.
	28.	Convince us that boredom is a gift.
	29.	Argue why doing nothing is sometimes productive.
	30.	You have one minute to inspire someone who wants to quit—what do you say?


How to Practice (2-minute winning routine)

For each topic:
	1.	5 sec – Pause & breathe
	2.	15 sec – Think: Past–Present–Future or Problem–Lesson–Application
	3.	1–1.5 min – Speak with examples
	4.	Last 10 sec – Strong closing line
